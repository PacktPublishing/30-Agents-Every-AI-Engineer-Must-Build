Acme Corporation — Corporate Compliance and Data Governance Policy
Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Document Classification: Internal — All Employees

1. Data Retention

All financial records, including invoices, receipts, tax filings, and audit logs, must be
retained for a minimum of seven (7) years from the date of creation. General business
correspondence, including internal emails and memoranda, must be retained for a minimum
of three (3) years. Automated deletion policies may be applied after the mandated retention
period, provided that deletion schedules are documented, approved by the Compliance
Officer, and auditable at all times. Departments are responsible for ensuring that retention
schedules are enforced within their document management systems.

2. Subscription Refund Policy

Customers enrolled in recurring subscription plans are entitled to a full refund if they
cancel within fourteen (14) calendar days of their most recent renewal date. Refund requests
submitted after the 14-day window will be prorated based on the remaining unused portion
of the subscription period. Prorated refunds are calculated as follows: the daily rate
(annual subscription cost divided by 365) multiplied by the number of remaining days in
the billing cycle. Refund processing takes 5 to 10 business days. No refunds will be issued
for promotional or discounted subscriptions unless required by applicable law.

3. Billing Terms and Dispute Resolution

All invoices are due within thirty (30) calendar days of issuance (Net-30). Late payments
are subject to a 1.5% monthly interest charge on the outstanding balance. Customers who
wish to dispute an invoice must submit a written dispute within fifteen (15) business days
of the invoice date. The Finance Department will acknowledge receipt of the dispute within
two (2) business days and provide a resolution within ten (10) business days. Disputed
amounts are not subject to late payment interest during the resolution period.

4. Access Controls and Data Security

Access to customer financial data is restricted to authorized personnel on a need-to-know
basis. All access events are logged and subject to quarterly audit review. Employees must
complete annual data protection training. Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary
action up to and including termination. External data sharing requires written approval
from the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) and must comply with all applicable
data protection regulations, including GDPR and CCPA where applicable.

5. Policy Amendments

This policy is reviewed annually by the Compliance Committee. Amendments take effect
thirty (30) days after publication to the corporate intranet. All employees will be notified
of material changes via email and are required to acknowledge receipt.
